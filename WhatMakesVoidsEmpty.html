Why are Voids so empty?

It is believed that in the Primordial universe (Or early universe) there were fluctuations of density that caused some areas to become denser than others.

Over billions of years, the denser areas began to drag in matter, leaving the lower density areas with almost nothing, thus creating the cosmic web Consisting of filaments of galaxies, gas and dark matter, the cosmic web is the web-like scaffolding of the universe. along with the voids we know today.

This also explains why voids remain mostly empty. Due to the low density within voids, it is incredibly difficult for matter to enter them.
