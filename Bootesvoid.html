Boötes Void

The Boötes Void is by far the most famous known void in our universe.

Located about 700 million light-years from Earth, sitting within The Boötes constellation (from which it takes its name), The Boötes void is a large spherical area spanning a massive 330 million light-years across.

To put it's size into perspective, a single light-year is approximately 9.46 Trillion kilometres. This means that The Boötes void is Three quadrillion, one hundred twenty-two trillion, forty-one billion, fifty-five million, nine hundred fifty-one thousand, six hundred sixty-four (3,122,041,055,951,664) kilometres wide!

Now, something important to note, The Boötes void (and majority of other voids) are not completely empty, they are still voids due to them having drastically lower densities of matter within them compared to the rest of the universe.

In the case of The Boötes void, it contains 60 or so galaxies, for any other area of that size it would be expected for it to contain around 2,000 galaxies.

So it's pretty empty?
