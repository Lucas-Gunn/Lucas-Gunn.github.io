What's a Void?

When you think of space you probably think of different planets and galaxies, some people dream about exploring a new planet or even a whole new solar system. But the part that people tend to forget about is how much of space is literally nothing, a complete void.

Voids are the aforementioned places of ‘nothing’ in space; voids are massive sections of space that are almost completely empty. Now, for a moment, sit there and imagine nothing, truly nothing; total darkness, complete silence, there is truly nothing, this is what fills these voids.

At this point in time we currently know of over 8,000 different voids, each separated by galaxy filaments, superclusters, and galaxy walls. It is believed that anywhere from 80-95% of space is made up of voids.

Among these voids is The Boötes Void, one of the largest supervoids Put simply, supervoids are exceptionally large voids, typically extending up to 300 million light years. we currently know of.
